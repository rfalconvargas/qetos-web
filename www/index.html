What the LDL tag means

A quick, plain-language guide to the tag we show on each meal — based on the kinds of fats and fiber a food contains.

↓

LDL-lowering (“removing”)

Foods generally associated with a friendlier LDL profile — soluble fiber, unsaturated fats (olive oil, avocado), omega-3s (fatty fish), nuts & seeds.

↔

LDL-neutral

Little measurable effect either way for most people — most non-starchy vegetables, eggs, and lean proteins.

↑

LDL-boosting (raises LDL)

Foods higher in saturated & trans fats — fatty cuts of red meat, butter, and processed or fried foods — tend to raise LDL for many people.
